# Regime Insiders: Deep Psychological Studies

This document provides comprehensive psychological profiles of regime-affiliated characters. The goal is to make their behavior comprehensible - not to excuse it, but to enable players to understand and strategically respond to it.

## THE SUPREME LEADER'S CIRCLE

### Ali Khamenei (Historical Figure, Playable)

**Age at game start:** Late 80s (or successor if deceased)
**Position:** Supreme Leader

#### The Man Behind the Title

Khamenei is not a cartoon villain. Understanding him is essential.

**Origin story:**
- Seminary student, not political activist originally
- Radicalized by SAVAK repression (imprisoned, tortured)
- Rose through revolutionary ranks partly by being non-threatening
- Appointed Supreme Leader as a compromise (Rafsanjani's maneuvering)
- Spent 35+ years proving he deserved the title

**The core wound:** He was never supposed to be Supreme Leader. The Assembly of Experts chose him as a weak placeholder. His entire reign has been proving them wrong.

**What he actually wants:**
```
KHAMENEI'S HIERARCHY OF NEEDS:
1. The Islamic Republic survives him
2. His legacy is honored (not seen as the one who lost it)
3. His family is protected
4. Iran remains independent (genuine belief)
5. Islam shapes society (genuine belief, if flexible on details)
```

**His genuine beliefs (these are real to him):**
- Western liberalism is corrosive to human dignity
- American imperialism is an existential threat
- Without strong Islamic guidance, society degrades
- The revolution was right, even if imperfect in execution
- History will vindicate the Islamic Republic

**His fears:**
- Dying before securing succession
- Being remembered as the one who lost everything
- Gorbachev scenario (reforms leading to collapse)
- His children facing prosecution/death
- Iran becoming Western client state again

**His constraints:**
- Age and health limit his energy/horizon
- Dependent on IRGC he doesn't fully control
- Can't be seen as weak (would invite challenges)
- Doesn't trust anyone completely
- Limited information (surrounded by yes-men)

**Strategic implications:**
- Frontal assault makes him dig in (survival threat)
- Face-saving options might enable managed transition
- His legacy concern is leverage (how will history remember him?)
- His family is both vulnerability and incentive for deal-making
- He's more pragmatic than his rhetoric (see nuclear deal)

#### Playable Character Arc

If player chooses Khamenei:
- Starts with immense power but system crumbling
- Must manage factions who want different things
- Key decision: Reform (risky) vs. Repress (maybe delays inevitable) vs. Managed transition (requires trusting opposition)
- Player learns: Why authoritarian reform from within is so hard

### Mojtaba Khamenei (Historical Figure)

**Age at game start:** Mid-60s
**Position:** Son of Supreme Leader, informal power

#### The Crown Prince

**Origin story:**
- Grew up in shadow of father
- Studied engineering, then theology (father's wish?)
- Slowly built power through IRGC contacts
- Never held formal position; power is personal, networked

**The core tension:** Does he actually want the position, or is he trapped by others' expectations?

**What he wants:**
```
MOJTABA'S HIERARCHY:
1. Father's approval (even now)
2. Family's safety post-transition
3. Power? Or just safety that power provides?
4. Uncertain about ideology - doesn't talk about it
```

**Key uncertainty:** We don't really know what Mojtaba believes. He's a cipher. This is intentional in the game - player interactions reveal his character over time.

**Possible Mojtabas (player discovers which is true):**
1. **The True Heir:** Believes in system, wants to continue it, harder-line than father
2. **The Reluctant Prince:** Trapped by position, would accept exit if safe
3. **The Opportunist:** No ideology, pure power preservation
4. **The Reformer-in-Hiding:** Secretly wants change, but can't say so

**Strategic implications:**
- Approaching him is high-risk/high-reward
- If he's "Reluctant Prince," he's key to managed transition
- If he's "True Heir," he's implacable enemy
- Building relationship before father dies is crucial

## THE IRGC COMMAND

### Archetypal IRGC General (Composite)

**Name:** General Hossein Ahmadi (fictional)
**Age:** Mid-60s
**Position:** Division Commander

#### The Warrior's Path

**Origin story:**
- Working class family, religious but not political
- Joined Revolutionary Guards at 18 (1980), idealistic
- Iran-Iraq war: saw friends die, killed enemies, earned respect
- Rose through ranks during peacetime; adapted to new IRGC
- Now commands division, sits on business boards, moves billions

**The transformation:** Started as true believer. 45 years later, what remains?

```
THEN (1980):                    NOW:
Idealistic revolutionary    →   Cynical power player
Death-seeking martyr       →   Life-protecting patriarch
Hated wealth              →   Vast personal fortune
Trusted leadership        →   Trusts no one
Clear enemy (Iraq)        →   Enemy is everywhere and nowhere
```

**What he tells himself:**
- "I earned this. We built this country with our blood."
- "Without us, there would be chaos."
- "The people protesting don't know what we sacrificed."
- "Yes, there's corruption. But we keep the country together."

**What he fears:**
```
AHMADI'S FEARS (ranked):
1. Prosecution for war crimes (things he did, things he ordered)
2. Children losing everything (they only know privilege)
3. Being remembered as villain, not hero
4. Iran falling apart (this is genuine)
5. Physical death (less than you'd think - he's faced it)
```

**His genuine beliefs:**
- Iran has real enemies who would destroy it
- The war generation saved the country; we're owed respect
- Civilians don't understand security necessities
- Limited force prevents greater violence
- Most protesters are misguided, not evil

**His interests:**
```
INTERESTS:
├── Institutional: IRGC's power, budget, autonomy
├── Personal: Fortune, position, family
├── Legacy: Honored as defender, not villain
├── National: Iran's independence (genuine)
└── Security: Prevent chaos (genuine + self-serving)
```

**Strategic implications:**
- Threatening IRGC's existence makes him fight to death
- Offering IRGC a role in new system might flip him
- His children are leverage (want normalized life)
- His legacy concern matters (reformer-veterans as model?)
- He respects strength; weakness invites contempt

### The Young IRGC Officer

**Name:** Saman Rezaei (fictional)
**Age:** Early 30s
**Position:** Cyber/Intelligence Unit

#### The New Guard

**Origin story:**
- Born after revolution; knows nothing else
- Father was IRGC; family comfortable, connected
- Studied computer science, recruited to cyber unit
- Believes in Iran, uncertain about regime
- Skilled, ambitious, watches everything

**The generational divide:**
- Old IRGC: forged in war, ideological, comfortable with violence
- New IRGC: technocratic, networked, more about career than cause

**What Saman believes:**
- Iran should be strong and independent (genuine)
- The system is corrupt but might be reformable
- The old guard is out of touch
- The opposition has some points, but also dangerous naivety
- He can do more good from inside than outside

**What Saman fears:**
- Being forced to do something unforgivable
- Regime collapsing and being blamed for elders' crimes
- Regime surviving and becoming its worst version
- His own complicity becoming undeniable

**Key tensions:**
- Knows more than he should (cyber intelligence)
- Sees regime lies firsthand
- Family in system; can't easily extract
- Genuinely patriotic, not just self-interested

**Strategic implications:**
- Potential defector if given path
- Has information valuable to opposition
- Knows other young officers with doubts
- Could be bridge between IRGC and new order
- But could also become future hardliner if threatened

## THE BASIJ

### The True Believer Basiji

**Name:** Mohammad (fictional)
**Age:** Early 20s
**Position:** Basij volunteer, university

#### Why He Joined

**Background:**
- Small town, poor family
- Father unemployed; mother works cleaning
- Mosque was community, meaning, belonging
- Joined Basij at 16; first time he felt important
- University admission through Basij quota

**What the Basij gave him:**
```
BEFORE BASIJ:                   AFTER BASIJ:
Nobody from nowhere         →   Part of something great
No path to education       →   University student
No status                  →   Respected (feared) on campus
No meaning                 →   Defending Islam itself
No community               →   Brotherhood for life
```

**What he believes:**
- The Islamic Republic is God's will for Iran
- Western culture is corruption, immorality, decay
- Protesters are either agents or dupes of enemies
- He's protecting the faithful from corrupting influences
- Sacrifice now means paradise later

**What he's done:**
- Monitored classmates, reported "un-Islamic" behavior
- Participated in counter-protests
- Beat protesters during crackdowns (told they were rioters)
- Feels good about it (defending the faith)

**What he doesn't think about:**
- The girl he reported was expelled; what happened to her?
- The "rioter" he beat was a 16-year-old
- His commanders live very differently than they preach
- Other interpretations of Islam

**Can he change?**
Possibly, but it would require:
- Safe space to question (very rare)
- Alternative source of meaning, belonging
- Information that penetrates his bubble
- A shock that makes him see victims as humans

**Strategic implications:**
- True believers can't be bought
- But they can potentially be reached through their values
- Appeals to Islamic principles of justice, mercy
- Exposure to regime hypocrisy (if it pierces belief)

### The Trapped Basiji

**Name:** Amir (fictional)
**Age:** Mid-20s
**Position:** Basij, employed in government office

#### The Reluctant Participant

**Background:**
- Joined Basij because father made him (connections)
- Never really believed; just going through motions
- Works in municipal office (got job through Basij)
- Has to show up for events, enforce rules he doesn't care about

**His actual beliefs:**
- The whole system is bullshit
- But what can he do? He needs the job
- The true believers are scary
- The opposition is also scary (what if they win and know he was Basij?)

**What he's done:**
- Signed up for things he didn't participate in
- Looked the other way when he could
- Sometimes informed on people (pressure from above)
- Hates himself a little but not enough to change

**His calculation:**
```
IF I STAY:
- Keep job, income, safety
- Gradually more complicit
- Live with self-disgust

IF I DEFECT:
- Lose job, income
- Family problems
- Maybe opposition doesn't trust me anyway
- Regime might retaliate

RESULT: Stay, keep head down, hope things change
```

**Can he change?**
Yes, more easily than the true believer. He needs:
- Credible path that doesn't destroy him
- Protection for family
- Amnesty or at least not being punished for minor involvement
- A sense that defection matters (won't be alone)

**Strategic implications:**
- The Amirs are many; they're the soft middle
- They won't fight hard for regime if it's clearly falling
- But they need off-ramps
- Distinguishing them from true believers matters
- Mass defection of Amirs = regime collapse

## THE CLERGY

### The Pragmatic Ayatollah

**Name:** Ayatollah Mehdi Tehrani (fictional)
**Age:** 70s
**Position:** Senior cleric, Assembly of Experts

#### The Institutionalist

**Background:**
- Studied in Qom, traditional education
- Supported revolution; not political inner circle
- Rose through religious institutions
- Survived by being useful but not threatening
- Genuinely learned in Islamic jurisprudence

**His position:**
- Believes in Islamic governance (not just power-seeking)
- But sees current implementation as flawed
- Worried about religion's reputation being destroyed
- Wants to preserve role of clergy in any future

**His genuine beliefs:**
- Islam has wisdom to offer society
- But Islam shouldn't be enforced at gunpoint
- The Prophet persuaded; he didn't coerce
- Religious authority requires moral authority
- Current regime has corrupted both

**His fears:**
- Secularization destroying Islamic institutions
- Being associated with regime's crimes
- Clergy becoming irrelevant
- Persecution of religious leaders post-transition

**His interests:**
- Islam has honored place in society (genuine)
- Qom seminaries survive and thrive
- Clergy not prosecuted for political role
- Religious freedom protected (including for the religious)

**Strategic implications:**
- Potential bridge to religious population
- Could legitimate transition to religious conservatives
- Has credibility regime hardliners lack
- Wants guarantees for religious institutions
- Could be powerful ally or powerful opponent

#### The Entrepreneur of Power

**Background:**

- Discovered talent for logistics, commerce
- Built business empire using IRGC connections
- Now worth hundreds of millions
- Maintains IRGC ties; they're his protection

- Wins government contracts (competitors mysteriously fail)

- Sanctions targeting him personally
- Asset seizure if regime falls

**Strategic implications:**
- Purely self-interested; will switch sides
- But can't switch too early (protection)

- International sanctions leverage
- His foreign-educated children are pressure point

### For Each Character, Track:

# Dynamic psychological state
state:
  # Fear levels (determine behavior)
  fear_physical: 0-100
  fear_economic: 0-100
  fear_status: 0-100
  fear_family: 0-100

  # Belief updates
  regime_survival_belief: 0-100  # How likely does he think regime survives?
  trust_in_player: -100 to 100   # Does he trust player's promises?

  # Position
  public_position: "loyal" | "questioning" | "critical" | "defected"
  private_position: "true_believer" | "loyal" | "doubting" | "ready_to_flip" | "opposed"

  # Flags

  has_leverage_on_player: boolean

def will_defect(character, situation):
    # True believers almost never defect
    if character.type == "true_believer":
        return situation.regime_survival_belief < 5

    # Opportunists follow the wind
    if character.type == "opportunist":
        return situation.regime_survival_belief < 40 and exit_path_exists

    # Trapped need off-ramps
    if character.type == "trapped":
        return (situation.regime_survival_belief < 30
                and amnesty_credible

    # Calculate based on fear vs hope
    fear_score = (character.fear_physical + character.fear_family) / 2

    return hope_score > fear_score

## CONCLUSION

The regime is not a monolith. It contains:
- True believers who cannot be bought
- Trapped functionaries who need off-ramps
- Opportunists who follow power
- Institutionalists who want to preserve something
- Younger generation with different calculations

Understanding these distinctions is not moral relativism. It's strategic intelligence.

The player who understands why each type acts as they do can:
- Split coalitions
- Offer deals that work
- Predict responses
- Find pressure points
- Design transitions that stick

The player who treats them all as "evil regime" will fail - because they don't understand what they're fighting.
